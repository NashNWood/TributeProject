Edgar Allan Poe

Master of the Macabre!

Here is a timeline of Poe's Life:

  • Jan. 19, 1809: Edgar Poe is born in Boston, the second child of two actors.
  • 1810: Abandoned by his father.
  • 1811: His mother dies of consumption (pulmonary tuberculosis).
  • 1811: Poe is taken in by Frances Allen of Virginia, receives middle name of Allan.
  • 1815: Poe and the Allan family sail to England.
  • Feb. 1826: Poe enrolls at the University of Virginia to study ancient and modern languages.
  • Apr. 1827: Poe has left the University of Richmond and travelled to Boston to work as a clerk and newspaper writer.
  • May 27, 1827: Poe joions the military to support himself at age 18.
  • Late 1827: He publishes Tamerlane and Other Poems, anonymously.
  • Apr. 15, 1829: Poe is discharged from the military. He publishes his second book.
  • March 1831: Poe returns to Baltimore to his remaining birth family.
  • Aug. 1, 1831: His brother dies due to complications of alcoholism.
  • Aug. 1835: Becomes assistant editor of Southern Literary Messenger in Richmond.
  • May 16, 1836: Marries his 14 year old cousin Virginia Clemm at the age of 27.
  • Jan. 29, 1845: "The Raven" appears in the Evening Mirror and becomes a popular sensation. Poe is a household name.
  • Jan. 30, 1847: Virginia dies from complications due to consumption.
  • 1847-49: Poe becomes increasingly unstable and becomes known for his drinking and erratic behavior.
  • Oct. 3, 1849: Poe is found delirious, in great distress on the streets of Baltimore.
  • Oct. 7, 1849: Poe dies at the Washington Medical College.
"Men have called me mad; but the question is not yet settled, whether madness is or is not the loftiest intelligence."
